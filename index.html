Frequently Asked Questions

I work with adults in individual therapy. My practice focuses on helping people navigate anxiety, trauma and PTSD, depression, stress, and major life transitions. I also support clients in working through challenges in all types of relationships, including those with family, friends, partners, and coworkers, while helping them build healthy boundaries and express their emotions more effectively.

You can reach out to me through my contact form to set up a quick 15–20 minute phone call. We’ll use that time to see if we’re a good fit and talk about what you’re looking for. If we decide to move forward, we’ll schedule an initial session. In that first meeting, we’ll explore what brings you to therapy, what you’d like to work on, and go over any questions you might have. From there, we can create a schedule that feels right for you.

Standard sessions are $160 and are typically 50 minutes. Extended sessions (75 minutes) are available as needed and are $240. I also hold a few sliding-scale spots for folks with limited financial access. I don’t bill insurance directly, but I’m happy to provide superbills for possible out-of-network reimbursement.

Therapy is a space where you don’t have to pretend or hold it together. It’s time set aside to slow down, talk things through, and explore what’s been going on in your life. We’ll look at thoughts, feelings, and patterns with curiosity, not judgment. When it aligns with your goals, I may incorporate EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing). EMDR is a research-backed method that helps process and heal from past difficult experiences or trauma by reducing the intensity of painful memories and opening up new ways to understand and cope with them.
